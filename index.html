I am an undergraduate researcher at Loyola University Chicago working at the intersection of formal methods and large language models — asking whether machines can write specifications a model checker will actually accept.

With the AI4FM / FMitF group under Prof. Konstantin Läufer, I built the first systematic evaluation of LLM-generated TLA+, trained the chattla-20b model, and presented ChatTLA+. Outside the lab I write compilers (Resilient), ship macOS/iOS apps, and keep 116+ repositories on the go.

Research Statement

Formal verification has guarded industrial systems at Amazon and Microsoft for years, yet writing a correct specification still takes an expert and a lot of time. If a language model could turn an English requirement into a spec that passes the checker, verification stops being a luxury. My work measures exactly how far today's models are from that — and tries to close the gap.
